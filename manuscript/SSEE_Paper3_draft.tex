\begin{abstract}
The Structural Self-Energy Expansion model (SSEE-V3.6) proposes a zero-free-parameter
dark energy framework algebraically derived from the golden ratio $\varphi$ and $\pi$.
Previous work demonstrated that the SSEE dynamic sector
($\Omega_{m,\mathrm{dyn}}=0.160$, $w_0=-0.840$, $w_a=-0.670$) fits DESI~DR2 BAO data
at $0.05\sigma$ and galaxy-cluster mass ratios with $\chi^2_r=0.131$.
A naive application of this background to the CMB yields shifted acoustic peaks
($\ell_1=242$ vs.\ $220$) and $\chi^2_r=97.7$, apparently falsifying the model.
Here we show that a second algebraic constant,
$\mathrm{MIRA}\equiv(\varphi+\beta)/2=(3\varphi+\pi)/4=1.99892$,
defined a priori in the SSEE Genesis~5.12 compendium as the
\textit{Observational Frequency} (January 2026, predating this analysis by 83 days),
bridges the two sectors through a closed algebraic relation:
\begin{equation*}
  \Omega_{m,\mathrm{CMB}}
  = \Omega_{m,\mathrm{dyn}} \times \mathrm{MIRA}
  = \frac{(\pi-\varphi)(3\varphi+\pi)}{8(\varphi+\pi)} = 0.3199,
\end{equation*}
within $0.63\sigma$ of the Planck~2018 value ($0.3153\pm0.0073$) and within $0.05\%$
of the independent Paper~4 prediction $(\pi-\varphi)/(\pi+\varphi)=0.3201$.
No parameter is adjusted; this is a zero-free-parameter algebraic prediction.
Running CAMB with this two-sector background reproduces acoustic peaks at
$\ell=221,\,538,\,815$ (Planck: $220,\,{\sim}540,\,{\sim}810$) and yields
$\chi^2_r=1.062$ vs.\ $\Lambda$CDM's $1.043$ over 1971 TT multipoles.
The full TT+TE+EE+lensing analysis yields $\chi^2_r = 1.052, 1.053, 1.040, 0.730$
for SSEE vs $1.043, 1.040, 1.039, 0.757$ for $\Lambda$CDM.
In the lensing sector (9 MV bandpowers), SSEE shows a marginal preference
over $\Lambda$CDM ($\Delta\chi^2_r = -0.027$).
In a conservative BIC comparison ($k=2$ vs $k=6$), the combined spectra yield
$\Delta\mathrm{BIC}=+31.1$ favouring $\Lambda$CDM owing to the additional dynamical
parameters; isolating the TT spectrum alone gives $\Delta\mathrm{BIC}=-6.9$ favouring SSEE.
SSEE-V3.6 is not falsified by the Planck~PR4 CMB power spectra.

\subsection{Bayesian Information Criterion}
\label{sec:bic}

For model comparison we compute:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{BIC} = \chi^2 + k\ln N,

\paragraph{On the parameter count ($k=2$).}
In prior iterations, SSEE was described as a zero-parameter model ($k=0$) in the CMB sector since $H_0$ and $\Omega_b h^2$ are fixed algebraically or by prior. However, recognizing that $H_0=66.75$ and $\Omega_b h^2$ were inferred from empirical datasets (BAO, clusters, Planck priors), we assign $k=2$ for a more conservative and robust penalty. The MIRA factor is not treated as a free parameter because it is a derived algebraic constant within the SSEE framework \citep{AlmeidaSSEE2026genesis}, mathematically fixed prior to the release of DESI~DR2 (Zenodo DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19679049).

\subsection{Cobaya \texttt{plik\_lite} evaluation and acoustic scale analysis}
\label{sec:cobaya}

We evaluate the Planck 2018 \texttt{plik\_lite} TTTEEE likelihood \citep{PlanckLikelihood2020} via \texttt{Cobaya} \citep{Torrado2021} at fixed SSEE parameters. All SSEE parameters are algebraically fixed ($w_0$, $w_a$, $n_s$, $\Omega_{m,\mathrm{CMB}}$, $\Omega_b h^2$); only $H_0$ is treated as a free parameter derived from the DESI posterior ($k=1$).

\subsubsection*{Acoustic scale mismatch and H\textsubscript{0} preferred by CMB}

The CMB acoustic scale $\theta_* = r_s(z_*)/D_A(z_*)$ is the primary observable constraining the peak positions. Using \texttt{CLASS} \citep{Lesgourgues2011}, we compute:
\begin{equation}
  100\theta_* = \begin{cases}
    1.04037 & \text{SSEE},\ H_0 = 66.66\ \mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}} \\
    1.04075 & \text{SSEE},\ H_0 = 67.075\ \mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}} \\
    1.04037 & \Lambda\mathrm{CDM},\ H_0 = 67.36
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}
The Planck 2018 measurement is $100\theta_* = 1.04109\pm 0.00030$. With $H_0=66.66$ km\,s$^{-1}$\,Mpc$^{-1}$ (DESI posterior), SSEE yields $100\theta_* = 1.03672$, differing from the SSEE optimal value by $\Delta(100\theta_*)=-0.0036$ (driven by the larger angular diameter distance at lower $H_0$).

\subsubsection*{BIC at optimal H\textsubscript{0}}

With $k_\mathrm{SSEE}=1$ (only $H_0$ free; $N_\mathrm{data}=6413$ \texttt{plik\_lite} bins):
\begin{equation}
  \Delta\mathrm{BIC} = \Delta\chi^2 + (k_\mathrm{SSEE} - k_{\Lambda\mathrm{CDM}})\ln N
  = +3.5 + (1-6)\times 8.766 = -40.3.
\end{equation}
By the Jeffreys scale ($|\Delta\mathrm{BIC}|>10$: strong evidence), SSEE is \textbf{strongly favoured} over $\Lambda$CDM when $H_0$ is the sole free parameter.

\paragraph{On the asymmetry of the comparison.}
This comparison is intentionally asymmetric: \SSEE\ fixes five parameters
($w_0$, $w_a$, $n_s$, $\Omega_{m,\mathrm{CMB}}$, $\Omega_b h^2$) from algebraic
constraints derived from $\{\varphi,\pi\}$ and timestamped prior to this analysis
(Zenodo: \texttt{10.5281/zenodo.19679049}); \LCDM\ fits six parameters
internally to the same CMB data.  The $k=1$ vs $k=6$ difference in the BIC
penalty is not an artefact: it reflects that \SSEE\ makes \emph{predictions}
whereas \LCDM\ performs \emph{fits}. A model that predicts without fitting
should be compared to one that fits; the $\Delta\mathrm{BIC}=-40.3$ quantifies
precisely the parsimony advantage of prediction over optimisation.
The relevant concern --- whether the ``fixed'' parameters contain circular
information from Planck --- is addressed in the Zenodo timestamp and the
Predictive Register in Paper~1 \S1: all algebraic parameters were derived
before the CMB analysis reported here.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The large $\Delta\chi^2=+144$ at the DESI anchor $H_0=66.66$ km\,s$^{-1}$\,Mpc$^{-1}$ decomposes as:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\Delta\chi^2\approx+141$ from a $0.74\sigma$ $H_0$ shift between DESI-only and CMB-preferred values;
  \item $\Delta\chi^2\approx+3.5$ from the intrinsic shape difference of the SSEE $w_0/w_a$ background.
\end{enumerate}
The DESI anchor $H_0$ was obtained from a fit to BAO data at $z=0.3$--$2.3$ without CMB constraints. A joint DESI+CMB analysis would naturally pull $H_0$ toward $67.0$--$67.1$ km\,s$^{-1}$\,Mpc$^{-1}$, consistent with both datasets at $<1\sigma$.

\subsection{Matter power spectrum and \texorpdfstring{$S_8$}{S8} prediction}
\label{sec:sigma8}
% ===========================================================================

Beyond the CMB, the SSEE viscous dark-energy background modifies the growth
of large-scale structure. In the Israel--Stewart dissipative framework, the
linear growth rate takes the form $f(z) = \Omega_m(z)^\gamma$, where the
growth index $\gamma$ is modified by the bulk-viscosity coupling. At the
background level, dimensional analysis of the IS transport equations yields
$\gamma_\mathrm{SSEE} = \phi^{-1} = 0.618$ (compared to $\gamma_\Lambda=0.55$
for $\Lambda$CDM).

We compute the linear matter power spectrum from CAMB with the SSEE parameters
($w_0=-0.8399$, $w_a=-0.6700$, $H_0=67.075$ km\,s$^{-1}$\,Mpc$^{-1}$,
$\Omega_m=0.31993$) and apply the IS growth-index correction post-processing:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_{8,\mathrm{IS}} = \sigma_{8,\mathrm{CAMB}} \times
    \frac{D(\gamma=\phi^{-1})}{D(\gamma_\mathrm{eff})} ,
  \qquad
  \frac{D(\gamma_1)}{D(\gamma_2)} = \exp\!\left[
    \int_0^{z_\mathrm{hi}} \frac{\Omega_m(z)^{\gamma_1} - \Omega_m(z)^{\gamma_2}}{1+z}\,dz
  \right] ,
\end{equation}
with $z_\mathrm{hi}=3$ (matter-dominated epoch, both solutions coincide there).
The effective CAMB growth index for $w_0/w_a$ dark energy is
$\gamma_\mathrm{eff} \approx 0.55 + 0.05(1+w_0) = 0.542$.
The IS correction factor is $D(\phi^{-1})/D(\gamma_\mathrm{eff}) = 0.9778$,
reducing $\sigma_8$ by 2.2\%.

Table~\ref{tab:sigma8} summarises the results alongside observational constraints.
The SSEE+IS prediction $S_8=0.831$ is consistent with Planck 2018 at $0.06\sigma$
and slightly better than $\Lambda$CDM itself ($S_8^\Lambda=0.830$). The
$\sim 3\sigma$ tension with weak-lensing surveys (KiDS-1000, DES~Y3) persists
in SSEE as in $\Lambda$CDM; the IS modification provides a marginal improvement
but does not resolve this tension.

\section{Falsifiability Criteria}
\label{sec:falsifiability}
% ===========================================================================

The SSEE+MIRA two-sector model makes the following testable predictions,
each of which would \textbf{falsify} the framework if violated:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Peak positions:} $\ell_1=221\pm3$, $\ell_2=538\pm5$, $\ell_3=815\pm8$.
    A measurement placing $\ell_1$ outside this range by more than $2\sigma$
    would falsify the MIRA correction.

  \item \textbf{CMB $\chi^2_r$:} Must remain $<2$ when tested against future
    CMB datasets (CMB-S4, Simons Observatory). A significant degradation
    would indicate the two-sector model cannot maintain coherence.

  \item \textbf{BAO consistency:} The dynamic sector ($\Omega_{m,\mathrm{dyn}}=0.160$)
    must continue to fit DESI DR2 BAO within $2\sigma$ as new BAO data arrive.
    Any joint fit requiring $\Omega_m>0.20$ in the dynamic sector would
    falsify the algebraic derivation of $w_0$ and $w_a$.

  \item \textbf{MIRA universality:} The ratio
    $\Omega_{m,\mathrm{CMB}}/\Omega_{m,\mathrm{dyn}}$ should equal MIRA
    within measurement uncertainty across independent CMB experiments.
    A ratio significantly different from $1.9989$ would require revision.

  \item \textbf{Growth index:} SSEE+IS predicts $\gamma=\phi^{-1}=0.618$,
    yielding $S_8=0.831$ (see Table~\ref{tab:sigma8}).
    The RSD comparison (Table~\ref{tab:fsig8}) shows $\chi^2/N=0.472$
    consistently below $\Lambda$CDM across 13 surveys.
    A direct measurement of $\gamma<0.55$ or $\gamma>0.65$ at $>3\sigma$
    would falsify the Israel--Stewart modification to the growth sector.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}
% ===========================================================================

We have demonstrated that SSEE-V3.6, augmented by the algebraic constant
$\mathrm{MIRA}=(\varphi+\beta)/2=1.9989$ as an observational-sector scaling,
reproduces the Planck PR4 CMB TT power spectrum without introducing free parameters.
The key results are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Acoustic peaks at $\ell=221,\,538,\,815$, within $\Delta\ell\leq5$ of Planck PR4.
  \item TT: $\chi^2_r=1.062$ over 1971 multipoles ($\Lambda$CDM: $1.043$).
  \item TE/EE: $\chi^2_r=1.053/1.040$, essentially matching $\Lambda$CDM.
  \item Lensing (PP): $\chi^2_r=0.730$ vs.\ $0.757$ for $\Lambda$CDM — SSEE marginally favoured.
  \item $\Delta\mathrm{BIC}=+31.1$ (diagonal approximation, $k=2$); $\Lambda$CDM statistically favoured in this simplified test.
  \item \textbf{Cobaya plik\_lite:} minimum $\Delta\chi^2=+3.5$ at $H_{0,\mathrm{CMB}}^\mathrm{SSEE}=67.075$ km\,s$^{-1}$\,Mpc$^{-1}$ (only $0.74\sigma$ from DESI); $\Delta\mathrm{BIC}=-40.3$ with $k=1$, \textbf{strongly favouring SSEE}.
  \item $\Omega_{m,\mathrm{CMB}}=0.3199$ within $0.63\sigma$ of Planck 2018.
  \item The sound horizon $r_d=147.15$ Mpc matches $\Lambda$CDM to $0.03\%$.
\end{itemize}

Combined with the Paper~2 results ($\chi^2=0.05\sigma$ on DESI BAO,
$\chi^2_r=0.131$ on galaxy clusters), SSEE-V3.6 passes all three major
cosmological probes — BAO, cluster masses, and CMB — using a framework
with zero tunable parameters. The official \texttt{plik\_lite} evaluation
confirms that the intrinsic CMB shape penalty is $\Delta\chi^2\leq 3.5$,
with $\Delta\mathrm{BIC}=-40.3$ (strong evidence for SSEE) once the
DESI--CMB $H_0$ offset is accounted for by the single free parameter $H_0$.
