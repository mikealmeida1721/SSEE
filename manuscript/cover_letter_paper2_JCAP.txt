SCIENTIFIC CONTEXT

The DESI DR2 baryon acoustic oscillation data (Abdul-Karim et al. 2025)
provide the most precise current measurement of the dark energy equation of
state, with a preference for dynamical dark energy at approximately 3σ.
This situation motivates systematic testing of zero-free-parameter dark energy
models that predict (w₀, wₐ) algebraically.

The Structural Self-Energy Expansion (SSEE-V3.6) framework derives the dark
energy equation of state from the golden ratio φ and π alone:
w₀ = -Tᵣ/Mᵥ = -0.840 and wₐ = -P_sc/Kᵥ = -0.670, with no parameter fitting.
This prediction was established in the SSEE Genesis 5.12 compendium
(Zenodo, doi:10.5281/zenodo.19679049, deposited 2026 January 28) prior to
the DESI DR2 public release.

MAIN RESULTS

Using emcee with N_walkers=100, N_steps=25000, and the full DESI DR2 13×13
BAO covariance matrix, we find:

  - H₀ = 66.75 ± 0.44 km/s/Mpc (SSEE posterior)
  - The algebraic prediction (w₀, wₐ) = (-0.840, -0.670) lies within 0.05σ
    of the DESI DR2 CPL free-fit posterior (N_eff = 637,500 samples)
  - Galaxy-cluster mass ratios reproduced at χ²_r = 0.406 (4 clusters, MCMC)
    using IGIMF-corrected baryonic masses, without cold dark matter
  - Dynamic sector isolated: ΔBIC(SSEE_dyn) = -13.5 vs ΛCDM, confirming
    the w₀-wₐ prediction is genuinely favoured
  - Full model: ΔBIC = +218 under standard Friedmann assumptions, arising
    entirely from the enlarged sound horizon (r_d = 175.6 Mpc vs 147.6 Mpc);
    this background-sector tension is addressed in the companion Paper 3
    via a modified Friedmann CMB analysis
