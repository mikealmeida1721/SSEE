SCIENTIFIC CONTEXT

A persistent challenge for zero-free-parameter dark energy models is
compatibility with the CMB acoustic scale, which encodes Ω_m at recombination.
The SSEE framework predicts Ω_m,dyn = 0.160, which naively shifts the first
acoustic peak from ℓ = 220 to ℓ = 242 and yields χ²_r = 97.7 — an apparent
falsification. This paper resolves this tension using a second algebraic
constant of the SSEE framework.

THE MIRA FACTOR AND PRIOR DEFINITION

The SSEE framework defines a structural constant MIRA = AURA/2 = (φ+β)/2,
where φ is the golden ratio and β = (φ+π)/2. This constant, with value
MIRA = 1.9989 ≈ 2, was established in the SSEE Genesis 5.12 compendium
(Zenodo, doi:10.5281/zenodo.19679049, initial commit 2026 January 28),
predating the DESI DR2 release and any CMB fitting in this work.
Its physical role as a "depth ratio" connecting the BAO and CMB observational
sectors is demonstrated in §3 of the manuscript.

MAIN RESULTS

Running CAMB with the two-sector background
Ω_m,CMB = Ω_m,dyn × MIRA = 0.160 × 1.9989 = 0.3199:

  - Acoustic peaks: ℓ = 221, 538, 815 (Planck PR4: 220, ~540, ~810)
  - TT power spectrum: χ²_r = 1.062 vs ΛCDM's 1.043 (N = 1971 multipoles)
  - TE polarisation: χ²_r = 1.053 vs 1.040 (N = 1967)
  - EE polarisation: χ²_r = 1.040 vs 1.039 (N = 1967) — essentially identical
  - CMB lensing (9 MV bandpowers): χ²_r = 0.730 vs 0.757 — SSEE marginally
    favoured
  - BIC comparison (k=0 SSEE vs k=6 ΛCDM), TT alone: ΔBIC = -6.9 favouring
    SSEE

SSEE-V3.6 is not falsified by any of the four CMB observables tested.
Three explicit falsifiability criteria are provided in §6.
